About

Research at the intersection of nutrition, health, and welfare.

I am a poultry scientist at the University of Georgia. My research connects nutrition, gut health, and physiology to the health, welfare, and resilience of poultry. During my PhD I examined how feed-borne mycotoxins, coccidial infection, and environmental stress reshape the intestinal barrier, immune function, skeletal development, and the gut microbiome in laying hens and broilers.

I work at the bench and in controlled challenge studies, pairing gut-permeability assays, qPCR, ELISA, and flow cytometry with DEXA and micro-CT imaging of bone and body composition. I am now extending this work toward management and welfare questions in modern poultry production.

I earned my PhD in Poultry Science at the University of Georgia, MS at the University of Arkansas at Pine Bluff, and a BS in Agriculture from Tribhuvan University in Nepal.

Research

Areas of focus

My work spans the gut, the skeleton, and the microbiome, with a growing focus on welfare and management in poultry production.

01

Gut Health & Intestinal Integrity

Intestinal barrier function under dietary and pathogen stress, measured through gut-permeability assays and histomorphology.

02

Mycotoxicology

Effects of feed-borne mycotoxins such as deoxynivalenol and aflatoxin B1 on growth, gut health, immunity, and bone.

03

Skeletal Biology & Body Composition

Bone mineralization and microarchitecture assessed by DEXA and micro-CT across nutritional and disease challenges.

04

Gut Microbiome

Diet- and infection-driven shifts in the cecal microbiome using QIIME2 and metagenomic approaches.

05

Immunology

Innate and adaptive immune responses to enteric challenge, characterized by flow cytometry and cytokine profiling.

06

Health, Welfare & Management

Translating nutrition and physiology research toward welfare and management in modern poultry production.
